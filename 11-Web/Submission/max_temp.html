Latitude vs. Max Temperature (F)


This plot correlates maximum recorded temperatures at each of the latitides of the data set. As expected, increase in latitude, which is north from the equator, brings a decrease in max temperature. The coldest max temperature recorded is aboout -20 degrees at about 60 degrees latitude.

In southern climates, temperature varies greatly on location. There are some clusters to note: at about the equater to about -20 degrees south, the max temperature is firmly between 60-100 degrees farenheit. At about -40 through -50 degrees south of the equator, there is another cluster of locations that have a max temperature between 40 and 60 degrees farenheit.
